A **relative clause** gives more information about a noun. It begins with a **relative pronoun** (*who, whom, whose, which, that*) or a **relative adverb** (*where, when, why*).

## Examples

:::example
The author **who wrote** this guide is a teacher.

This is the café **where** we first met.

The reason **why** she left was never explained.
:::

## Defining vs non-defining

- **Defining** — essential to identify the noun; no commas.
- **Non-defining** — extra information; commas (or pauses in speech).

*My brother who lives in Berlin is visiting.* (defining — which brother)

*My brother, who lives in Berlin, is visiting.* (non-defining — one brother)
